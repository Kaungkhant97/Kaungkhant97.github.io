Nay Pyi Taw

Nay Pyi Taw is the capital city of Myanmar. The 24th and 25th ASEAN Summit are held in Nay Pyi Taw. It was also one of the host cities for the 2013 Southeast Asian Games.

Yangon

Yangon is the commercial city of Myanmar. A mix of colonial architecture, modern high-rises and glided Buddhist pagodas define its skyline. Shwe Dagon Pagoda is the famous tourist attraction

Sagaing

Sagaing is the capital fo Sagaing Region in Myanmar. It's located on the Irrawady River. Sagaing with numerous Buddhist monasteries in an important religious and monastic center.

Mandalay

Mandalay is the former royal capital in northern Myanmar on the Irrawaddy River. In its center is the restored Mandalay Palace from the Konbaung Dynasty , surrounded by a moat.

Bagan

Bagan is an ancient city located in Mandalay Region. From 9th to 13th centuries, the city was the capital of the Kingom of Pagan. It is Myanmar's most famous tourist attraction.

Kyaik Hto

Kyaik Hto is a city in Mon State. Kyaiktiyo Pagoda is the famous tourist attraction near Kyaikto. It is a small pagoda built on the top of huge rock and that balancing rock seems to defy gravity.

Pyin Oo Lwin

Pyin Oo Lwin is a scenhic hill town located in Mandalay region. It is famous for its nice weather. It is also known as "Flowers City". The various colors and species of flowers can be founded everywhere in this city.

Nyaung Shwe

Nyaung Shwe is a city located in Shan State. It is a part of Shan Hills. Inlay Lake, one of famous attractions of Myanmar, is located near Nyaung Shwe.It is a beautiful highland lake.

Lashio

Lashio is the largest town in northern Shan state. It is located on a low mountain spur overlooking the vallty of the Nam Yao river. It is famous for its beautiful and breathtaking landscape view.
